# Entry 6 : MVP
##### 05/4/26

# Content
I've been focused on creating the MVP for my Freedom Project website about my topic which is business technologies. My MVP includes multiple webpages (sequals), navbar, buttons, image carousels, and cards describiing the  different software and hardware technologies there are in the business industry that they use today and in the near future. Additionally, while making my MVP I used HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, and Bulma. I first created the homepage with a navbar and a image carousel, then I made more pages for Existing Technologies and Future Technologies, so all the information wouldnt be mushed in into one page. Moreover, I added information about technologies like Slack, Canva, Wave, Penscanner, Portable Printer, and Ticktime.

# Challenges
One challenge I experienced while creating my MVP was making the website fit correctly on my Chromebook screen. The layout looked fine on my monitor but some sections looked too large on smaller screens. Another challenge was organizing all my pages and making sure the navbar links worked correctly.

# EDP (Engineering Design Process)
I am currently in the prototyping stage of the Engineering Design Process. My MVP is the first working version of my project, and I will continue improving the design and adding more features later.

# Skills

**Problem solving**

Problem solving has helped me fix alot of coding mistakes that I had made and layout issues on how it would look like in different screen sizes while building my website.

**Creativity**

Creativity helped me choose layouts, fonts, and images to make my website look more professional and gave me a opportunity to showcase my imagination on how my ideal website would look like.

# Summary
Overall, creating my MVP helped me improve my coding skills and understanding of web design. I learned more about HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, and how websites are organized. I also learned that testing and fixing mistakes is an important part of creating a successful website.
